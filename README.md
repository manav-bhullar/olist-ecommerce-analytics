# Olist E-Commerce Customer & Operations Analytics

## The Business Problem
Olist is a Brazilian e-commerce marketplace. This project investigates why customers are not returning and what operational factors are hurting revenue. The analysis focuses on customer retention through RFM segmentation and evaluates operational bottlenecks like delivery delays and seller performance.

## Data Source
[Brazilian E-Commerce Public Dataset by Olist](https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/olistbr/brazilian-ecommerce)

## Technical Stack
- **Python:** Data Cleaning, EDA, RFM Segmentation (`pandas`, `numpy`, `matplotlib`, `seaborn`)
- **SQL:** Revenue & Category Analysis (`duckdb`)
- **Tableau Public:** Final Seller Performance Dashboard

## Project Structure
- `notebooks/`: Contains all Jupyter notebooks for the step-by-step analysis.
- `data/raw/`: Raw CSV files from Kaggle.
- `data/processed/`: Cleaned datasets and exports for Tableau.
- `scripts/`: Helper utilities.

## Key Insights & Business Recommendations

1. **Delivery Delay Impact:** Delivery delays are severely hurting customer satisfaction. Orders that are delayed by 3+ days see their average review score drop by over 50% compared to on-time orders (from ~4.3 down to ~2.1).
2. **Logistics Audit Recommendation:** Sellers located in the São Paulo (SP) state account for a disproportionate amount (~40%) of the delayed orders. We highly recommend a regional logistics and carrier routing audit for the SP fulfillment nodes.
3. **Customer Retention:** RFM segmentation revealed that the "At-Risk" segment makes up 23% of the customer base. These customers haven't purchased in over 6 months but were historically frequent buyers. We recommend a targeted email re-engagement campaign offering free shipping to win them back.
4. **Top Categories:** The top-performing product categories (e.g., Bed Bath Table, Health Beauty, Sports Leisure) are responsible for over 60% of the total Gross Merchandise Value (GMV).
